How It Works

How does NexTransfer work?

Let’s look at an example to help us better understand NexTransfer.

Mark, an American software engineer working in Hong Kong, needs to send money to the U.S. in order to pay off his university student loans. On the other end, , there’s Julia, a paralegal working in Los Angeles, US, who would like to wire money for her sister's wedding in Hong Kong.

As long as Mark and Julia trust each other, they can mutually benefit from one another. Mark and Julia would each transfer a prior agreed sum of money to designated bank accounts within their respective countries. That is to say, Mark would wire X HKD to Julia’s sister in Hong Kong, and Julia would wire the equivalent X HKD in USD (X’ USD) to Mark’s U.S. bank account. In doing so, they both save on the fees that banks would normally charge upwards of 8.0% of the total amount.

How I trust the opposite person

When you find a person who can exchange the destination of the money with you, probably, the first thing comes in your mind is ‘how I can trust this person’.

NexTransfer, fundamentally, is a network built on trust. We verify people via email, social accounts and a passport and show their transfer record which indicates how fast they response and transfer money. Moreover, we hold a credit card for a case of ‘no-show’ (no-transfer) like a hotel. So even the case that the opposite person doesn’t send money to your designated account we guarantee your money safely sent to the destination.
