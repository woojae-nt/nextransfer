Why NexTransfer

You've Actually Been Paying Too Much

Many expats and students who work or study abroad end up paying significant fees when they transfer money internationally through banks and money operators. We at NexTransfer believe that this is unfair.
We are so convinced of this injustice that we are building a network where people can exchange their currency at the mid-market rate, or the mid-point of the 'BUY' and 'SELL' spread. These are the rates that large institutions traditionally only had access to. But we’re striving to bring them to everyone as part of our philosophy of financial inclusion. This makes up our ethos.
























Why do you accept buying high and selling low? The delta (difference) between the “buy” price and “sell” price (known in finance as the “bid/ask spread”) is pocketed by the intermediary.


A Free and Fast Transfer by Exchange

How does NexTransfer work? Let’s look at an example to help us better understand. 

Mark, an American software engineer working in Hong Kong, needs to send money to the U.S. in order to pay off his university student loans. On the other end, there’s a young lady by the name of Julia, a paralegal working in Los Angeles, US, who would like to wire money for her sister's wedding in Hong Kong. 

As long as Mark and Julia trust each other, they can mutually benefit from one another. Mark and Julia would each transfer a prior agreed sum of money to designated bank accounts within their respective countries. That is to say, Mark would wire X HKD to Julia’s sister account in Hong Kong, and Julia would wire the equivalent X money in USD to Mark’s American student bank account. In doing so, they both save on the fees that banks would normally charge upwards of 7.9% of the total amount.
